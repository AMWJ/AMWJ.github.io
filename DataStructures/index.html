About this page

This page shows 3 equivalent data structures:

  • Red-Black Trees
  • 2-3-4 B-Trees
  • Deterministic Skip Lists
While each looks different than one another, and maintain their data in different ways, they each perform the same operations, and have analogous structure. (If the Red-Black Trees were forced to be left-leaning, all 3 would be isomorphic to one another). Sources:
  • Red-Black Trees can be found on Wikipedia, as well as many places online.
  • The Wikipedia page on 2-3-4 Trees was also helpful
  • Deterministic Skip Lists are defined by Munro, Papadakis, and Sedgewick here.
